01 / Principles

Six principles that hold the product together.

Principles are decisions, not decoration. Each one describes a visible choice a designer can defend and a reviewer can reject.

P · 01

Schedule truth over marketplace noise.

Availability, duration, and price are authoritative or honestly absent. We never suggest a slot we cannot honor.

Not: browse-first, editorial discovery, "shops you might love".
P · 02

Repeat, not rediscover.

The product opens on last groomer, last pet, last service. One tap rebooks. Discovery is secondary, not hero.

Not: infinite feeds, featured shops, weekly editors' picks.
P · 03

Trust is a spoken promise.

Card holds, deposits, release windows and cancellation actors are explained in plain language at the moment of commitment.

Not: hidden fees, fine-print modals, surprise authorisations.
P · 04

Request-confirm is a first-class flow.

High-variance cases route to request-confirm with the same care as instant booking. It is not a degraded fallback.

Not: a generic "contact merchant" detour.
P · 05

Thai-first, English-ready.

Thai script is a primary citizen in layout, type scale, and line rhythm. English is the considered companion, not the canonical source.

Not: localisation as an afterthought.
P · 06

Operationally serious, humanely warm.

Merchant and ops surfaces are dense without hostility. Consumer surfaces are warm without cartoon.

Not: gamified UI, emoji mascots, Figma-template polish.

07 / Alternate directions

One recommended. Two on the table.

The prompt asks for a premium-editorial and an operational-product alternate alongside the primary. All three are built from the same token layer, so migration is a hue and weight change — not a rebuild.

Recommended

Primary · The Merger

Editorial serif accents on warm paper with operational density at the product layer. Reads premium in the deck, reads dense in the merchant board, reads warm in the customer app. One system, no fork.

Use for · customer app, merchant ops, control plane, CEO deck, one-pager.

Alt A · Premium editorial

Leaning editorial.

Paper goes warmer, Fraunces takes more hero moments, body goes to a longer line length. Feels magazine-premium; photos of real shops carry the weight.

Risk · merchant board gets precious; dense data feels hostile against a warm background.

Alt B · Operational product

Leaning operational.

Inter Tight takes every hero moment, paper goes cooler, serif is reserved for a single deck slide. Merchant board hums; customer home reads slightly more utilitarian.

Risk · CEO deck loses editorial warmth; the brand starts to read like a B2B SaaS admin.

Recommendation

Ship the merger. The editorial system is too soft for the merchant board, and the operational system does not earn the CEO deck. The merged system lets the same team demo customer, merchant, ops, and investor decks without switching brands mid-walkthrough.

What a pivot costs later
  • Primary hue shift · one token value · cascade across all surfaces
  • Serif swap · one font face · 6 usage sites to recheck
  • Paper tint shift · one oklch hue parameter
  • Density ramp · switch density class on merchant and ops surfaces only

Alternates are not trashed — they remain available as named token sets so a future segment (enterprise merchant, b2b payouts) can adopt one without a second design effort.
