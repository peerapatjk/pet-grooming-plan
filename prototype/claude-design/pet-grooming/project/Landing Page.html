Desire, built from calm clarity

Trust is a feeling until the flow makes it visible.

Pawpoint makes routine grooming feel booked, not promised; reviewed, not mysterious; and repeatable, not re-entered.

The emotional job of the product is simple: remove the soft uncertainty that makes grooming appointments feel provisional. Each scroll card below maps to a specific trust surface in the app.
Card hold, not hidden charge.

The moment that usually creates friction becomes legible. Customers see whether the app is reserving a slot with a hold, asking for a small deposit, or doing nothing at all. The point is not stricter policy. The point is cleaner expectation.

Every pending state comes with a clock and a branch.

When a merchant needs to review a case, the app names the expected response time and what happens if that time runs out. Pending becomes a managed state instead of a blank wait.

Repeat booking becomes the quiet payoff.

Once a pet, merchant, and stylist relationship exists, the app should feel almost invisible. That is the real moat against messaging workflows: not novelty, but the way the next appointment starts halfway done.

For grooming shops

One canonical schedule, not six soft promises.

The merchant side matters because public trust only holds when the schedule is operationally credible. Pawpoint is designed to be the board the shop actually uses, not a second inbox beside LINE.
Truthful
Availability comes from the merchant.

Offline
Phone, walk-in, and chat bookings land on the same board.

Offline capture is part of the product, not an afterthought. That keeps the merchant from maintaining a parallel reality outside the app.

Safe
Review flows expire cleanly and stay auditable.

Approve, decline, or let the timer close. Nobody should be wondering whether an expired request can quietly come back from the dead.

Launch wedge

A tight start, so the trust model can hold.

The landing page should make the slice feel intentional, not small. Narrow scope is the point: it is how Pawpoint avoids becoming another generic pet marketplace with weak booking semantics.
Where

Central Bangkok, dense neighborhoods first.

Sathorn, Ari, Thonglor, Phrom Phong, and nearby sois where premium independent grooming already lives close to home.

Not yet: suburbs, other cities, broad marketplace sprawl
Who

Independent shops with repeat clientele.

Owner-operated grooming studios that already care about craft and service quality, but still run too much of the schedule through chat memory.

Not yet: chains, franchises, low-trust coverage promises
What

In-shop grooming, one pet, one truthful slot.

Baths, tidy work, full grooms, nails, ear care, and a controlled set of fixed add-ons. Simple enough to schedule honestly. Valuable enough to earn repeat.

Not yet: home service, multi-pet bundles, broad concierge detours
