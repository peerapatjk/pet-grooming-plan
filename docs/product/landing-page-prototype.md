# Landing Page Prototype Brief

## Purpose

Create a clickable prototype for a static marketing landing page that explains the product clearly and drives users to download the app.

This is not a web booking product. The landing page exists to:

- explain the value proposition
- build trust
- frame the launch wedge
- send users to download the customer app

## Prototype Goal

Validate that the product can be introduced cleanly through a premium static site without accidentally creating a second, half-baked web booking flow.

## Primary Flows To Cover

1. Understand the product in under 10 seconds
2. Understand who it is for
3. Understand why it is different from chat-based booking
4. Understand the trust model at a high level
5. Click through to download the app

## Critical Constraints

- static site only
- direct to App Store / Play Store style CTAs
- no booking functionality
- no web onboarding flow
- no web search flow
- no parallel account experience
- no hidden pseudo-product buried in the landing page

## Prototype Sections

### Section 1: Hero

- clear headline
- concise supporting copy
- product visual or phone mockup
- primary CTA: download app
- secondary CTA: learn how it works

### Section 2: Problem

- current booking behavior is fragmented and manual
- chat-based coordination is slow and unreliable
- rebooking is too manual

### Section 3: What the product does

- fast routine booking
- request-confirm for high-variance cases
- repeat booking for the same pet
- trusted merchant scheduling

### Section 4: Why users trust it

- clear booking states
- payment protection explained simply
- reminders and reconfirmation
- no-show reduction and schedule clarity

### Section 5: Why merchants care

- truthful availability
- less back-and-forth
- offline booking capture
- one canonical schedule

### Section 6: Launch wedge

- Bangkok-first
- in-shop grooming only
- premium-leaning independent shops first

### Section 7: Download CTA footer

- App Store / Play Store style CTA area
- FAQ or trust footer if useful

## Critical Questions To Test

- Can someone understand the product quickly without seeing the full app first?
- Is the page persuasive without overpromising?
- Does the page make it clear that booking happens in the app, not on the website?
- Does the trust language increase confidence rather than create fear?

## Success Criteria

- Visitors can explain what the product does in one sentence.
- Visitors understand that the app is the primary product surface.
- Visitors understand the difference between routine instant booking and exception handling at a high level.
- The CTA to download the app feels natural and strong.

## Failure Signals

- Users expect to complete a booking directly on the website.
- The page feels like a generic pet marketplace.
- The page promises coverage or features beyond the launch slice.
- The page over-indexes on visual polish without enough trust explanation.

## Moderator Script

- Ask the viewer what they think the product does after the hero section.
- Ask whether they think booking happens on the site or in the app.
- Ask what makes the product feel trustworthy or untrustworthy.
- Ask whether they would tap the download CTA.

## Notes To Capture

- first-impression clarity
- expectation mismatch about site vs app
- trust questions
- CTA strength
- launch-slice comprehension

## Output

The prototype review should produce concrete recommendations for:

- hero message
- trust framing
- merchant-value framing
- launch-wedge clarity
- CTA placement and wording
