# CEO Demo Deck Prompt

## When To Use

Paste this into Claude Design to generate a **leadership-ready deck** that explains the product strategy, launch slice, and why this is worth building now.

## Prompt

```text
Create a polished executive presentation deck for the CEO and senior stakeholders of a Bangkok-first pet grooming booking platform.

This is not a fundraising fantasy deck. It is a product and operating narrative for a focused launch slice.

The presentation should explain:
- the problem
- why current workflows are broken
- why the launch wedge is strategically tight
- what the product experience looks like
- why this is more than "AI can make pretty screens"
- what needs to be validated before deep implementation

Target audience:
- CEO
- Product / GM / Launch DRI
- Operations
- Finance / Accounting
- Support / Merchant Success
- Marketing / Sales / BD
- Tech leadership

Tone:
- sharp
- confident
- evidence-oriented
- high-judgment
- premium and boardroom-ready

Avoid:
- hypey AI-tool chest-thumping
- vague "marketplace for pets" framing
- generic startup clichés
- too much tiny text

Use this product thesis:

We are not building a broad "Chope for pets" clone.
We are building a repeat-first, trust-first grooming booking product for Bangkok that:
- makes routine bookings very fast
- routes high-variance cases to request-confirm
- uses payment protection to reduce fake bookings and no-shows
- gives merchants one canonical schedule they can actually trust

Core product truths:
- mobile-first customer surface
- desktop/tablet-first merchant surface
- in-shop only
- one pet + one primary service template per booking in the launch slice
- instant booking for narrow routine services only
- request-confirm for complex or higher-risk cases
- card hold default for routine bookings
- deposit for higher-risk or higher-value cases
- no waitlist, no native reschedule, no multi-pet bundle flow in V1

I want the deck to include:

1. Cover slide
- strong title
- concise subtitle
- premium visual framing

2. Why now
- fragmented booking behavior is still normal in this category
- mobile behavior is normal
- no-show controls and merchant trust create immediate value

3. The customer problem
- chat-based coordination is slow, unclear, and unreliable
- rebooking is too manual

4. The merchant problem
- stale availability
- shadow scheduling
- no-shows
- constant coordination overhead

5. Our wedge
- Bangkok-first
- premium-leaning independent grooming shops
- busy urban pet owners
- repeat-first thesis

6. What we are actually building
- customer journey
- merchant operations system
- payment trust layer

7. Why hybrid booking matters
- routine instant booking
- request-confirm for edge cases
- why pretending everything is instant would destroy trust

8. Trust and schedule truth
- provisional holds
- merchant response timing
- payment protection
- explicit cancellation semantics

9. Customer prototype showcase
- visually embed or simulate the strongest 2-3 moments from the customer flow

10. Merchant prototype showcase
- visually embed or simulate the strongest 2-3 moments from the merchant flow

11. Why repeat booking is the real moat
- saved pet context
- faster rebooking
- merchant trust + history

12. Launch slice vs later roadmap
- what is in
- what is out
- what is deferred

13. Risks and what must be validated
- service taxonomy
- request-confirm triggers
- provisional-hold expiry
- merchant SLA
- onboarding minimum fields
- customer tolerance for payment protection

14. Discovery and pilot plan
- concierge pilot
- clickable customer prototype
- clickable merchant prototype
- analytics and evals

15. What success looks like
- first-booking clarity
- schedule-truth thresholds
- payment-protection tolerance
- merchant daily-use willingness
- repeat-booking signal

16. Closing slide
- why this launch slice is strategically coherent
- what decision we want from leadership next

Visual guidance:
- make this feel like a top-tier product strategy deck
- elegant but operationally grounded
- strong use of product screens, not walls of text
- use diagrams, side-by-side states, and clean framework slides
- wherever possible, convert complexity into visual models

Narrative requirement:
- make it obvious that the product is compelling because of workflow integrity, not because AI happens to be involved
- position Claude Design-generated artifacts as a fast way to explore and communicate the product, not as the strategy itself

Please generate:
- the full deck
- speaker-note style captions or annotations on key slides
- a version that can be shown directly to the CEO

Make the final deck feel like it was produced by a very strong product organization with good design taste and strong operational judgment.
```

## Good Follow-Ups

- "Make slide 7 and 8 more visual and less text-heavy."
- "Show the launch slice versus deferred roadmap as a cleaner matrix."
- "Tighten this for a 7-minute CEO review."
- "Make the closing ask sharper and more decision-oriented."
